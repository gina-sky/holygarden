\*\*The Neuroplasticity Of The Brain

大脑的神经可塑性\*\*

I am repeatedly heartened to read the accumulating evidence from neuroscience research that proves the neuroplasticity of the brain. _Neuroplasticity_ means that the brain can grow and change throughout our life. Old self-destructive neural pathways can be diminished and new healthier ones can replace them. _A General Theory of Love_ by Thomas Lewis inspiringly explicates this fact. This fact helps me to remember that the critic can indeed literally be shrunk via longterm, frequent and dedicated use of the thought-stopping, thought-substitution and thought-correction practices.

我反复感到欣慰，阅读神经科学研究积累的证据，这些证据证明了大脑的神经可塑性。神经可塑性意味着大脑可以在我们的一生中不断生长和变化。旧的自毁性神经通路可以被削弱，新的更健康的神经通路可以取而代之。《爱的艺术》一书中，托马斯·刘易斯富有启发性地阐述了这一事实。这一事实帮助我记住，通过长期、频繁和专注地使用思维停止、思维替代和思维纠正的实践，批评者确实可以被实质性地缩小。

As recovery progresses, you notice the critic sooner before its attack becomes multidimensional. This then allows you to take more immediate self-protective action. Moreover, Cptsd flashbacks [[null|]]can be utilized as evidence of, and in later stages of recovery, proof of your childhood traumatization. Flashbacks point irrefutably to the fact that your parent’s abandonment forced you to habituate to hypervigilance and negative noticing.

随着恢复的进展，你会在其攻击变得多维之前更早地注意到批评者。这让你能够采取更迅速的自我保护行动。此外，Cptsd 闪回可以用作童年创伤的证据，并在恢复的后期阶段证明你的童年创伤。闪回无可辩驳地指向这样一个事实：你父母的遗弃迫使你习惯于过度警觉和负面注意。
