\*\*OUTER CRITIC MODELLING IN THE MEDIA

媒体中的外部批评建模\*\*

Outer critic entrenchment is also difficult to dislodge because its parlance is normalized, and worse, celebrated in our society. Skewering people seems to be standard practice in most TV comedies. Moreover, many influential, seemingly healthy adults model a communication style that is rife with judgmentalness, sarcasm, negativity, fear-mongering and scapegoating.

外部批评的根深蒂固也难以动摇，因为它的用语在我们的社会中已经规范化，更糟糕的是，它还被庆祝。在大多数电视喜剧中，讽刺人们似乎是一种标准做法。此外，许多有影响力、看似健康的成年人模仿的交流风格充满了评判性、讽刺、消极、恐吓和推卸责任。

Giving control of our social interactions to the outer critic prohibits the cultivation of the vulnerable communication that makes intimacy possible. We must renounce unconscious outer critic strategies such as: \[1\] “I will use angry criticism to make you afraid of me, so I can be safe from you”; \[2\] “Why should I bother with people when everyone is so selfish and corrupt” \[all-or-none thinking\]; \[3\] “I will perfectionistically micromanage you to prevent you from betraying or abandoning me”; \[4\] “I will rant and rave or leave [[null|]]at the first sign of a lonely feeling, because ‘if you really loved me, I would never feel lonely’”.

将我们社交互动的控制权交给外部批评家，阻碍了培养使亲密关系成为可能的那种脆弱的沟通。我们必须摒弃无意识的批评策略，例如：\[1\]“我将使用愤怒的批评让你害怕我，这样我就可以从你那里得到安全”；\[2\]“为什么我要费心与人交往，因为每个人都这么自私和腐败”（全有或全无的思维）；\[3\]“我将完美主义地微观管理你，以防止你背叛或抛弃我”；\[4\]“我会在感到孤独的第一迹象时大发雷霆或离开，因为‘如果你真的爱我，我永远不会感到孤独’”。
